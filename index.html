BOOK REVIEW

"Abel wanted a traditional wife... but he never fell in love with subservient women... 'He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage."

This quote from the book reflects the complex interaction of the society to gender roles and the difference between the traitional expectations and the modern personal freedom. Trevor's step father Abel's desire to have a traditional wife while desiring to put her in cage reflects the contradictory expectations that exist with in a relationship. the longing for a woman who is free and idependent but his actions being controling and demanding reflects the societal expectations of the gender roles. In modern society, there is still a conflict between gender roles and that of the old traditional expectations. Women are expected to be independent, assertive, and smart in many parts of the world, yet a lot of men still expect women to conform to traditional roles of being bounded and submission in relationships. This contradiction is visible not only in romantic relationships but also in work and public life, where women’s role is often undermined by society to conform to gender norms. The idea of wanting someone who is free, yet desiring to cage them, mirrors real-life power struggles. It speaks to men who may say they support women’s independence but at he end of the day want to control them in some wether it is emotionally, financially, or socially. This speaks about the undeniable truth in today’s society even in the age of equality, many women still face the burden of balancing their independence with societal expectations of submission. I’ve seen this in relationships around me, where partners may claim to support equality but impose outdated gender roles. The challenge, therefore, is not just about women fighting for their freedom, but also about men recognizing that true freedom for both partners is not about control but mutual respect and understanding.
